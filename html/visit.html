Getting There

Directions & Transport

✈

By Air

Fly into Siem Reap International Airport (SAI). Direct connections from Bangkok, Singapore, Kuala Lumpur, Ho Chi Minh City, and Phnom Penh.

🚲

From Siem Reap

Angkor Wat is 6 km from Siem Reap town. Options: tuk-tuk (~$10/day), bicycle hire (~$2/day), guided minibus tours, or private car.

🗺

On Site

The complex is large — 2+ km from entrance to central tower. Hire a local guide ($25–40/day) for an incomparably richer experience.

Insider Knowledge

Essential Visitor Tips

What should I wear to visit Angkor Wat?

Angkor Wat is an active religious site. Visitors must cover their shoulders and knees to enter. Light, breathable fabrics in neutral colours are ideal for the heat. Remove shoes before entering inner sanctuaries — wear sandals that are easy to slip on and off. Avoid white clothing as it shows dust easily.

When is the best time to see the sunrise?

Arrive at the reflecting pool west of the main entrance around 5:00–5:30 AM for the iconic sunrise. The towers glow in shades of amber and rose as the sun rises behind them, creating a perfect reflection in the still water. Bring a torch and arrive before 5:15 AM to secure a good spot, especially during peak season (November–February).

How much time do I need?

To see Angkor Wat itself properly — all three levels, the bas-reliefs, and the towers — plan for a minimum of 3–4 hours. Many visitors spend a full day. For the wider Angkor complex including Angkor Thom and Ta Prohm, plan 2–3 days. A 7-day pass allows a deeply immersive experience at your own pace.

Is a guide necessary?

Not required, but highly recommended. The stories behind the bas-reliefs, the astronomical alignments, and the mythological symbolism are incredibly rich — a knowledgeable local guide transforms the experience from sightseeing to revelation. The official Angkor Guide Association provides certified guides at the ticket office. Expect to pay $25–40 USD per day.

What should I bring?

Essential items: plenty of water (2L+ per person), sunscreen and a wide-brimmed hat, insect repellent, comfortable walking shoes, a small daypack, cash (USD is widely accepted), and a camera or phone fully charged. A sarong is useful for covering up quickly and can be purchased at the entrance. A small flashlight helps in darker inner chambers.

Are there food and water facilities on site?

Yes. There is a designated restaurant zone south of the main temple, open 7 AM – 6 PM, offering Khmer and international food. Vendors sell cold water, coconuts, and snacks along the main causeway. Bring your own water and top up as needed. Avoid buying food from unlicensed sellers inside the temple itself.
