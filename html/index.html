The Wonder

A Universe Built in Stone

Commissioned by King Suryavarman II in the early 12th century, Angkor Wat is the world's largest religious monument — a masterwork of classical Khmer architecture dedicated first to Vishnu, then transformed into a Buddhist shrine.

Spanning over 400 acres with a moat that mirrors the cosmic ocean, its five towers represent Mount Meru — the sacred axis of the Hindu cosmos. The walls are adorned with the world's longest continuous bas-relief: 800 metres of carved narrative depicting divine battles, celestial dancers, and the mythology of creation.
